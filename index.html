NFL Play Analysis: Rushing vs Passing

Explore the strategic dynamics of NFL play-calling through five interactive visualizations. This analysis reveals how teams balance between passing and rushing plays across different game situations, field positions, and scoring scenarios.

Play Distribution

This visualization shows the basic distribution of play types across the NFL. Compare the frequency of passing versus rushing plays to understand teams' overall offensive strategies. Use the filters to explore how these patterns vary by team and year.

Situational Success

Analyze how teams perform on different downs. This visualization breaks down the success rates of passing and rushing plays by down number, revealing how teams adapt their strategies as they progress through each set of downs.

Scoring Situation Analysis

See how the score differential influences play-calling decisions. This chart reveals how teams adjust their pass/rush balance when leading or trailing, highlighting strategic adaptations under different scoring pressures.

Play Outcome Distribution

Examine the distribution of yards gained for both passing and rushing plays. This density plot shows the relative frequency of different gain amounts, helping identify which play types are more likely to result in specific yardage outcomes.

Game Situation Impact

Understand how game clock and field position combine to influence play-calling. This visualization reveals how teams balance risk and reward in different game situations, showing the relationship between time remaining, field position, and play selection.
